Super Mario Galaxy completely redefined what a platformer could be for me, utilizing mind-bending gravity mechanics and a breathtaking orchestral soundtrack that made every planet feel like an epic adventure. Super Mario Odyssey built on that 3D perfection by giving me unparalleled freedom to explore vast, vibrant kingdoms, using Cappy to possess enemies and discover secrets in every corner. More recently, Super Mario Bros. Wonder captured my heart all over again with its wild, unpredictable Wonder Flowers and incredibly expressive animations, proving that classic 2D Mario can still surprise and delight me after all these years.

Celeste is a true masterpiece; not only does it feature incredibly tight, satisfying platforming, but its beautiful narrative about confronting anxiety and self-doubt resonated with me profoundly. Undertale completely flipped my expectations of traditional RPGs, showing me a world where every choice matters, humor blends perfectly with melancholy, and sparing enemies is just as viable as fighting them. Finally, I have a very special place in my heart for Mago, a charming retro 2D platformer developed by Dream Potion Games and spearheaded by the YouTuber Pepe el Mago. Its intuitive controls, gorgeous pixel art, and challenging boss fights perfectly capture the golden age of arcade gaming, earning it a permanent spot on my favorites list.
